I studied Computer Science and Engineering at HKUST from 2017 to 2021, which means I may know a thing or two about problem solving and computing in general. It does not mean that I magically know how to fix your computer problems! ;-)

During my study at HKUST, I developed a few areas of interest:

  • Interactive theorem proving (ITP): the art of constructing sound logical arguments with the assistance of specialized computer software known as proof assistants
  • Formalization of (pure) mathematics: a subset of ITP where mathematical proofs are encoded in a format that allows it to be objectively verified by a machine, thus gaining absolute confidence in its correctness
  • Program analysis: reasoning about the properties and correctness of a program
  • Type theory: the design of type systems, their properties and implications

I haven't dabbled much in these areas since undertaking formal employment, but if you happen to share similar interests, feel free to contact me and we can discuss about it! I do happen to know a few resources and communities related to these fields.
